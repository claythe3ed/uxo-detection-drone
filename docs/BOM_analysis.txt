BOM Analysis - UXO Detection Drone
========================================
Based on Blueprints.am design (extracted 2026-06-06)

- Flight Controller: Holybro Pixhawk 6C ($200)
- Payload MCU: Arduino Mega 2560 ($40)
- GPS: u-blox ZED-F9P RTK ($250)
- Magnetometers: Two HWT3100-485 fluxgate ($150 each)
- Laser Altimeter: LightWare LW20/C ($220)
- Power Distribution Board + BEC ($40)
- Battery: 4S 5000mAh LiPo ($80)
- Motors: 4x 2212 920kV ($15 each = $60)
- ESCs: 4x 30A ($10 each = $40)
- Telemetry Radio: Holybro SiK ($60)
- Frame: DJI F450 carbon fiber plates + arms ($~35)
- Propellers: 4x 1045 ($3.5 each = $14)
- Sensor Boom: Carbon fiber telescopic pole ($25)
- 3D printed mounts (total ~ $12)
- Misc: nylon standoffs, screws, cable ties (~ $8)

Total Estimated Cost: $1405.58 (as per Blueprints.am)

Compatibility with Simulation Requirements:
- Flight altitude 1 m: supported (altimeter provides precision)
- Speed 1 m/s: achievable with 2212 motors at low throttle
- Endurance: 4S 5000mAh estimated 15-20 min with payload (matches requirement)
- Magnetometer sensitivity: 13 nT/LSB (matches Maggy paper)
- Thermal camera: not included in BOM (assumed separate add-on, e.g., FLIR Lepton)
- Boom length: 30-50 cm non-magnetic (explicitly listed)

Recommendations:
- Add thermal camera module (FLIR Lepton 3.5 ~$250) to enable thermal detection.
- Consider upgrading battery to 4S 8000mAh for longer endurance (>25 min).
- Ensure 3D printed mounts use non-magnetic filament (PETG, PLA).
- Calibrate magnetometer boom length to minimize interference from drone electronics.
